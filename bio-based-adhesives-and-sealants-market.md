<p><p>The bio-based adhesives and sealants market consists of various types derived from renewable resources. Rosin material, extracted from pine trees, offers excellent adhesion properties and is widely used in pressure-sensitive adhesives. Starch material, sourced from crops like corn and potatoes, provides a biodegradable option suitable for packaging applications. Lignin material, a byproduct of wood processing, enhances durability and thermal resistance. Soy material, derived from soybeans, presents an eco-friendly adhesive with strong bonding capabilities. Lastly, other raw materials include proteins and cellulose, broadening the market's sustainability scope.</p></p>
