<p><p>Point-to-multipoint communication systems encompass two primary market types: Wide Area (Macro) and Small Cell (Metro). Wide Area systems provide extensive coverage, often utilizing high-power base stations to serve large geographical regions, making them ideal for rural and suburban areas. In contrast, Small Cell systems are designed for denser urban settings, where numerous low-power base stations facilitate high-capacity connections in localized areas. This structure enhances network efficiency and user experience in crowded environments, catering to increasing data demands.</p></p>
